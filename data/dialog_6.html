Your policy

You can add up to 3 other people on your insurance.

Adding another driver can make your insurance cheaper, because insurers think theres less risk with a car thats shared. It's really important to correctly identify the main driver of the car as its illegal to put someone as the main driver if they only drive the car occasionally. The main driver is the person who drives the most miles in the car.

Why do we ask

Insurers need to know if you or any other driver on the policy has ever had an insurance policy declined, cancelled, voided or had special terms imposed.

There are three types of car insurance policy to choose from:

Fully comprehensive

  • The highest level of cover.
  • Will pay out if the insured vehicle is lost, stolen or damaged.
  • Will pay out to fix any other vehicle involved in an accident with the insured vehicle.
  • Will provide cover for injury to a third party and for damage to property in an accident.

Third party, fire and theft

  • Will pay out for damage or loss due to fire or theft.
  • Will pay out to fix other cars involved in an accident with the insured vehicle.
  • Will provide cover for injury to a third party and for damage to property in an accident.

Third party

  • The minimum amount of cover you can have.
  • Will pay out to fix other cars involved in an accident with the insured vehicle.
  • Will provide cover for injury to a third party and for damage to property in an accident.

Excess is a sum of money you'll pay to contribute towards the full cost of your claim.

This question is about voluntary excess. Don't worry, the value you give to us here will be clearly visible when you get your results, and can always be changed if need be.

Voluntary excess is an amount that you choose. It'll be added to the compulsory excess. Generally, the more voluntary excess you choose to pay, the lower your insurance premiums. This is because an insurer won't have to pay out as much if you do need to make a claim.

Compulsory excess is determined by your insurer and will differ per policy. This is the amount you'll have to pay towards the cost of your claim. Remember, your voluntary excess is added to the compulsory excess to get a total amount.

Wondering how to find out?

Details of your no claims discount should be on your current insurance documents.

Please bear in mind that you can only use your no claims discount on one car or insurance policy at a time.

Some insurance companies wont accept your no claims discount if you earned any or all of it on a van or a motorcycle, company car, fleet insurance or outside the UK, so check before you enter details.

You can spread the cost of your policy by choosing monthly payments, but this usually makes the total cost of cover higher than it would be if you paid in one go.

That's because the insurer is effectively lending you the money to pay for the policy, so it will charge you interest on the repayments you make across the year.

This means opting to pay monthly can cost up to 28%* more over the whole year than if you choose to pay annually. This does vary between insurers.

If you opt to pay monthly, the insurer might also run a credit check as you'll be taking out a "finance agreement". If you've got a low credit score, the insurer might charge a higher interest rate, so your repayments could be even higher.

So if you can afford it, paying for your policy annually is normally the cheapest way to get covered.
