Optional extras

Would you need help covering legal fees and access to expert legal assistance for motoring disputes if you were involved in an accident that wasn't your fault?

You'll generally get help if your claim has a greater than 50% chance of success and, as a minimum, you'll be offered £50k of legal costs towards the recovery of uninsured losses. Some insurers will also offer a smaller level of cover to help with legal costs if you are being prosecuted following an accident, again this is where there's a greater than 50% chance of success.
Please let us know if you would like to add legal cover

Would you need help covering medical expenses, lost income, and rehabilitation costs if you were injured in an accident?

It's important to remember that the lump sum you'll be paid will differ depending on the extent of the injury. Some insurers may cover passengers in the vehicle, and you might also be offered further upgrade options through the provider's site to increase the level of cover should you want it.
Please let us know if you would like to add personal injury cover

Would you need roadside assistance, towing services or options to continue your journey if your car broke down?

There's a range of breakdown options available for varying costs from each insurance provider, but as a minimum, they'll offer roadside assistance and then local towing within a ten-mile radius if your car can't be fixed at the roadside. It's worth noting that standard policies don't typically offer to come and fix your car within a 1-mile radius of your home, however, you can often opt for upgrade options through the provider's site for things like homestart, home rescue, onward travel and European cover.
Please let us know if you would like to add breakdown cover

Would you need a replacement vehicle if your car was being repaired after an accident?

As standard, many providers will offer a small hatchback for you to drive over a set period of time while your own car is being repaired, subject to availability. You can often opt for upgrades through the provider's site, including cover for written off vehicles. You can also upgrade to get a like-for-like replacement car. You might find that some insurers request that your own car is fixed by one of their approved repairers.
Please let us know if you would like to add a courtesy car
